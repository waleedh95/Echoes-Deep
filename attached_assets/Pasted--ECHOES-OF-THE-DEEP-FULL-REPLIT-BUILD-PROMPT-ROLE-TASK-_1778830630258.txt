## FULL STORY & NARRATIVE

### Protagonist: ELIAS
- Elias is a deep-sea rescue pilot in his 40s.
- He is calm, methodical, and speaks in short professional sentences.
- He believes he is on an official rescue mission to save a family trapped in a research submarine in the Mariana Trench.
- His HUD displays four face icons (his family) but their faces are blurred and pixelated — he cannot identify them clearly.
- As levels progress, his dialogue fragments reveal he is suppressing traumatic memories.

### The Truth (Hidden from player until the end)
- Elias is actually in a coma in a hospital. He has been for 10 years.
- His family died in a boating accident 10 years ago. He could not save them.
- The entire game — the ocean, the submarine, the rescue mission — is his comatose mind constructing a world where he CAN save them, replaying the trauma endlessly.
- The "Sound-Hunters" (enemies) are the rhythmic sounds of his hospital ventilator and EKG machine bleeding into his dream.
- The wireframe ocean is his brain's fragmented, half-conscious reconstruction of reality.

### Story Beats Per Level

**LEVEL 1 — The Descent**
- Elias descends into the trench. Comms are fuzzy.
- He finds the first survivor: the MOTHER (Sara). She is unconscious. He docks with her lifepod.
- Cutscene (comic panel): A blurry memory — a family on a boat, laughing. Elias's hand reaches for someone but the image cuts to black.
- Elias whispers: *"I found one. I'll find the rest."*

**LEVEL 2 — The Pressure Zone**
- Deeper. More enemies. Tighter corridors.
- Elias finds the ELDEST SON (Noah), trapped under debris. He must solve a sound-based puzzle to free him without attracting enemies.
- Cutscene (comic panel): A clearer memory flash — a child's drawing of a submarine. The word "DAD" written in crayon. The image cracks like glass.
- Elias: *"You're safe now. I've got you."* (His voice breaks slightly.)

**LEVEL 3 — The Abyss (Final Level)**
- Oxygen is at 20% and dropping. Two lifepods remain deep in the trench.
- Inside: the YOUNGER SON (Liam, age 7) and the DAUGHTER (Mia, age 5).
- Elias reaches both pods but his oxygen cannot sustain two rescue docks.

### THE CLIMACTIC CHOICE (Final Gameplay Moment)
The player sees two glowing lifepods side by side. A UI prompt appears:

**[E] DOCK WITH POD A — Save Liam (Oxygen: 12% remaining)**
**[Q] DOCK WITH POD B — Save Mia (Oxygen: 12% remaining)**
**[R] REROUTE SUIT OXYGEN — Save Both (Elias will not survive)**

**ENDING A — Save One Child:**
- Elias docks with one pod. The other pod's light goes dark.
- He surfaces. The wireframe ocean begins glitching heavily.
- Cut to: A hospital room rendered in washed-out whites and greys. Elias is lying in a bed. A nurse adjusts a ventilator. The rhythmic enemy breathing sound IS the ventilator — seamless audio transition.
- A doctor says quietly: *"His brain activity just spiked. Then flatlined."*
- Final comic panel: A newspaper clipping. "Man, 38, remains in coma after failing to save family in boating accident. Pronounced brain-dead today."
- Screen fades to black. Text: *"He saved one. In his mind, that was enough to finally let go."*

**ENDING B — Sacrifice (Save Both):**
- Elias reroutes oxygen. His HUD flickers. His vision blurs.
- Both pods lock in. Children's voices (distorted, dreamy): *"Dad?"*
- The wireframe ocean dissolves completely into white.
- Cut to: The hospital room. A heart monitor flatlines.
- The nurse gasps. Then — silence.
- Final comic panel: The same newspaper clipping — but pinned to a corkboard in a child's bedroom. Two crayon drawings beside it. Two submarines. Two stick figures inside.
- Text: *"He saved them all. Even if only in the place that mattered."*

## GAMEPLAY MECHANICS — FULL DETAIL

### 1. ECHOLOCATION (THE VISION SYSTEM)
- The screen is pure black at all times by default.
- When a sonar ping is emitted, a circular wave expands outward from Elias.
- Every piece of geometry the wave touches lights up as a neon wireframe for 3–5 seconds then fades back to black.
- Ping types:
  - **Small Ping:** Radius 150px, fades in 3 seconds, Noise +5
  - **Large Ping:** Radius 350px, fades in 5 seconds, Noise +25
  - **Flare:** Continuous small pings every 1.5 seconds from flare position for 8 seconds total, Noise +3 per ping
  - **Boost Noise:** While Shift held, Noise +2 per second passively
- Wireframe colors:
  - Walls/terrain: Cyan (#00FFFF)
  - Lifepods: Green (#00FF88), pulsing glow
  - Data logs: Yellow (#FFD700)
  - Enemies (when pinged): Red (#FF3333), jagged distortion effect
  - Elias's submarine: Always visible, soft blue glow

### 2. NOISE METER
- Displayed on HUD as a horizontal bar labeled "ACOUSTIC SIGNATURE"
- Range: 0–100
- Decays by 5 points per second passively (silence lowers it)
- Thresholds:
  - 0–30: SAFE (green bar)
  - 31–60: CAUTION (yellow bar, enemies become alert — move faster)
  - 61–80: DANGER (orange bar, enemies home toward noise source)
  - 81–100: CRITICAL (red bar, all enemies in level converge on Elias immediately)

### 3. OXYGEN SYSTEM
- Displayed on HUD as a circular gauge labeled "O2"
- Starts at 100% per level
- Depletes at 1% per 3 seconds (normal movement)
- Depletes at 1% per 1 second (boost active)
- Depletes 15% instantly on enemy contact
- When O2 hits 0%: Screen goes dark, Elias whispers *"I'm sorry..."*, game over screen with option to restart level
- Docking with a lifepod restores 20% O2

### 4. MOVEMENT PHYSICS
- Elias's submarine has simulated momentum — acceleration and deceleration, not instant stop/start
- Moving through tight corridors requires careful speed control
- Walls are solid collision objects
- Flares sink downward slowly after being dropped (simulated gravity in water)

### 5. PUZZLE MECHANIC (LEVEL 2)
- Noah's pod is trapped behind a debris field
- Three sound-emitting objects are in the area, each producing noise at different volumes
- The player must navigate to each object and interact (E) to silence them one by one
- But silencing each one ALSO dims the wireframe visibility (less ambient sound = less echolocation)
- A Sound-Hunter patrols the area — the player must time interactions between its breathing cycles
- When all three objects are silenced: debris field disintegrates (wireframe shatters), Noah's pod floats free

- **Environmental details:** Wireframe fish school in background (cosmetic). Broken submarine wreckage. Floating debris.
- **Oxygen challenge:** Should complete with 60–70% oxygen remaining if played carefully
- **Tutorial prompts:** "TAP LEFT CLICK to ping. Hold for a larger ping. Stay quiet."

### LEVEL 2 — THE PRESSURE ZONE
- **Shape:** Maze-like with multiple dead ends, forcing careful exploration
- **Width:** Some corridors as narrow as 1.5x submarine width — boost is risky
- **Key locations:**
  - ZONE A: Two STALKER enemies patrol crossing paths. Must time movement through gap.
  - ZONE B: The puzzle room — 3 noise objects, 1 Stalker patrol
  - END: Noah's pod after puzzle is solved
- **Environmental details:** Crumbling rock walls. Bioluminescent particles (cosmetic, faint). Pressure cracks leaking bubbles.
- **Oxygen challenge:** Should arrive at Noah's pod at ~40% oxygen if played well

### LEVEL 3 — THE ABYSS
- **Shape:** Wide open cavern — deceptively spacious, but the VOID LEVIATHAN fills it
- **Key locations:**
  - ENTRY: Elias receives final comm from both children simultaneously — Liam panicking, Mia humming
  - CENTER: VOID LEVIATHAN circles the entire cavern slowly. Its path blocks direct routes to both pods.
  - POD A (LIAM): Left side of cavern. Closer to entry.
  - POD B (MIA): Right side of cavern. Further. Requires passing near Leviathan.
  - CHOICE POINT: When Elias reaches one pod, the prompt appears for the final decision.
- **Oxygen challenge:** Player arrives at approximately 20% oxygen — the choice must be made quickly
- **Leviathan mechanic:** When Leviathan breathes, it emits a pulse that temporarily disrupts Elias's sonar (all wireframes go dark for 1 second). This happens every 8 seconds.

- Mobile: NOT required — desktop keyboard/mouse only
