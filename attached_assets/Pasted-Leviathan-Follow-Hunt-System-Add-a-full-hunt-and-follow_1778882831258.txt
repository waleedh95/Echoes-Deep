Leviathan Follow & Hunt System

Add a full hunt and follow behavior system to the Leviathans (Sound-Hunters) in Echoes of the Deep.

HOW LEVIATHANS DETECT THE PLAYER:
	The Leviathans are blind. They cannot see the submarine. They hunt entirely by sound.

Sounds that attract them:
	- Sonar ping (small) — attracts any Leviathan within medium range
	- Sonar ping (large) — attracts every Leviathan in the entire level
	- Colliding with rocks or walls — attracts any Leviathan within close range
	- Moving at full speed — creates a low constant noise that attracts nearby Leviathans slowly over time
	- Firing a flare — creates a loud burst that pulls all nearby Leviathans toward the flare location instead of the player — use this as a distraction

Sounds that do NOT attract them:
	- Moving slowly (half speed or less)
	- Staying completely still
	- Docking with a pod quietly


LEVIATHAN STATES — HOW THEY BEHAVE:

State 1 — Patrolling:
	- The Leviathan moves in a slow fixed patrol path
	- It is not aware of the player
	- Its body pulses dim and slow — like a heartbeat at rest
	- The ambient sound it makes is low and distant

State 2 — Alerted:
	- The Leviathan heard something
	- It stops its patrol and turns toward the sound source
	- It moves toward the last place it heard the noise
	- Its body pulses faster — brighter
	- The sound it makes gets louder and closer
	- If it reaches the sound location and finds nothing — it returns slowly to patrol after 5 seconds
        - If Leviathan notices you, he should make a very loud noise.

State 3 — Hunting:
	- The Leviathan has located the submarine directly
	- It locks on and follows the player continuously
	- It accelerates the closer it gets
	- Its body pulses rapidly — almost blinding
	- The sound becomes the loud mechanical breathing — the ventilator sound — overwhelming and close
	- It does not stop. It does not give up. It follows until it hits the submarine or loses the signal.

State 4 — Attacking:
	- The Leviathan reaches the submarine and rams it
	- Hull integrity drops significantly on impact
	- The screen shakes hard
	- The Leviathan pulls back slightly then immediately begins hunting again
	- It will keep attacking until the player escapes its range or distracts it with a flare


HOW THE PLAYER ESCAPES:
	- Move slowly and quietly — the Leviathan gradually loses the signal
	- Fire a flare in a different direction — it breaks off and chases the flare
	- Hide behind a large rock or wall — breaks line of sound temporarily
	- Go completely still for 8 seconds — the Leviathan loses the signal and returns to patrol


IMPORTANT DETAILS:
	- The Leviathan never fully disappears from the level — it always returns to patrol after losing the player
	- Multiple Leviathans do not coordinate with each other — they each react independently to sound
	- The closer a Leviathan is, the louder the ventilator breathing sound becomes — this is the player's audio warning system
	- There is no radar showing Leviathan position — the player must use sound alone to know how close they are
	- When a Leviathan is hunting, the analog gauge panel flickers slightly — the instruments react to the pressure of its presence


THE FEELING:
The Leviathan is not a monster. It is a sound. It is the machine keeping Elias alive, hunting down the last fragment of his mind still fighting to survive. Every time it gets close — that sound, that breathing — is the hospital room bleeding into the dream. The player should feel it before they see it. And they should always be afraid of silence turning into that sound."
